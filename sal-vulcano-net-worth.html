From 'Impractical Jokers' to Comedy Tours

Publish date: 2024-01-29

Sal Vulcano’s net worth is currently $7 million in 2023. He made his fortune from diverse sources, including comedy, acting, podcasts, business, and more.

Vulcano started his entertainment career as a comedian during his days in high school. Even though he studied finance at the university level, he still went back to the entertainment world after graduation and today, he is regarded as one of the most hilarious comedians of our time.

How Much is Sal Vulcano Worth?

The most recent estimation of Sal Vulcano’s net worth pegs it at a tidy $7 million. The exact amount he rakes in on a monthly and annual basis has never been captured, but he started making money very early in life.

Vulcano’s Income Sources

Sal is one American entertainer who is shrewd enough to diversify his sources of income. The New York native earned his fortune as a comedian, actor, TV producer, and podcaster.

He Started Earning the Big Bucks From Comedy

It became glaring that Vulcano had talent in comedy as far back as his days in high school, where he met and got acquainted with his three future co-participants of The Tenderloins, whom he followed into the improv club.

The trio, James Murray, Joe Gatto, and Brian Quinn, joined efforts with Sal Vulcano to launch the popular comedy troupe The Tenderloins in 1999. It was through The Tenderloins that they started producing the series known as “Impractical Jokers” which airs on truTV. They often leverage hidden-camera-style format in the makings of their productions. The show experienced its big break in 2011. During its first season, Impractical Jokers attracted over 30 million views, and to date, it is still going strong.

The group’s job in The Tenderloins involved shooting comedy sketches, which they uploaded via YouTube, Metacafe, and MySpace. Through their sketch entitled Time Thugs, the group won the “It’s Your Show” competition on NBC in 2007, which took them to another level of fame.

Talking about the monetary side of his comedy career, Sal Vulcano reportedly makes between $30k to $50 thousand per episode of the Impractical Jokers show.

Game Shows and Podcasts are Other Income Earning Avenue for Sal Vulcano

The Tenderloins cast members started appearing in game shows in 2019, starting with “The Misery Index.”

In 2020, truTV launched “Impractical Jokers: Box of Challenges” – a board game that revolves around the “Impractical Jokers” series. The distribution contract was handled by Wilder Games. This contributed to Sal’s bottom line, but the details are not known.

Talking about podcasts, Sal and his group run “The Tenderloins Podcast” and also “What Say You?”

He has one Movie Credit

In 2020, Impractical Jokers was finally made into a film entitled “Impractical Jokers: The Movie,” and Sal Vulcano starred in it alongside other members of his comedy troupe. The movie was reportedly produced with an initial budget of $3 million but went on to gross over $10.7 million when it hit the box office.

Sal Vulcano Once Tested the Wheeling-dealing World of Business

At a point in his career life, Sal Vulcano decided to go into business. That was when he invested in the New York City-based music and comedy club, The Full Cup.

The spot has existed for many years, and its ownership has changed hands several times. However, after acquiring the club, alongside his partners, it was besieged by many legal and financial issues accrued from the past owners.

After Sal noticed that it was going to be a rough ride, he was shrewd enough to sell off his shares in 2014, and the bar eventually closed down a couple of years later in 2016.

Sal’s timely exit saved him from making a monumental loss from the business, though it still dented his bottom line a bit.
